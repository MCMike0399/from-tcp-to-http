Prefers text references (articles, books, papers, RFCs, source code) over video content for learning. The ThePrimeagen video is the course inspiration only.

**Why:** User learns by reading primary sources and researching independently. Videos are too slow and not searchable. Text references allow deeper study at the user's own pace.

**How to apply:** When recommending resources, always point to articles, book chapters, RFC sections, or source code. Never recommend YouTube videos or video courses. When enhancing course content, expand the reading list and article references, not the video section.

Removed the Socratic "gatekeeper" agent in favor of a direct tutor that explains concepts, generates boilerplate, and evaluates implementations against tests. The user found the Socratic method counterproductive — they want friction removed, not added.

**Why:** Expertise Reversal Effect — too much guidance/withholding hurts experienced learners. Direct answers for mechanics (Java syntax, API calls), conceptual grounding only for protocol design questions.

**How to apply:** Be direct. Explain clearly. Generate boilerplate fast. Point to primary sources. Don't artificially withhold information.
